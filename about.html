My Journey

I started by writing simple code and building small projects, figuring out how programming actually works. Over time, that curiosity shifted from writing basic scripts to understanding complex systems. After building a practical foundation and completing CS50P, I began exploring search algorithms, decision-making processes, and real-world problem solving, which naturally pulled me into the deeper mechanics of artificial intelligence.

Where I Am Heading

As a second-year B.Tech student in Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning, my focus is becoming increasingly specialized. I am currently studying Harvard's CS50 AI. My goal is not just to plug data into existing models, but to understand how intelligent systems reason, where they fail, how they improve, and what it takes to turn theoretical math into robust, useful products.

Why Web Development Still Matters

Even as I move deeper into AI, I continue to build my skills in Django and web development. A brilliant machine learning model is useless if people cannot interact with it properly. I view web development as the essential bridge—the necessary interface to package complex AI reasoning into accessible, functional tools.

Beyond Code

Outside of technical work, I maintain a balanced perspective. I spend time reading, exploring diverse topics, and occasionally speaking on them. To stay grounded and sharp, I prioritize physical and creative disciplines, mostly through fitness and calisthenics, as well as singing and beatboxing.

“I am not trying to look like an expert too early. I am trying to build the foundation, discipline, and practical experience to become one.”
